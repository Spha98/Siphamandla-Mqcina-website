C4Me

C4Me was represent South Africa in pitching their
innovative solution for a brighter student life in Toronto,
Canada this December. Inspired by an interaction with a
visually impaired school-mate, Tshwane University of
Technology students Siphamandla Mqcina and Philanjalo
Ndlovu came up with the idea of C4Me, a device that will
assist the visually impaired to find their way through campus
and any building with a mapping system. They entered this
idea into Red BullBasement University – a global platform
for students to innovate and co-create positive change – and
has led them to be selected to represent South Africa at the
upcoming Red Bull Basement University Global Workshop 2019
in Toronto, Canada this December where teams from 27
countries will pitch their ideas to improving student life.

This project will help people with visual impairments to move
freely, know exactly where they are and where they are going.
